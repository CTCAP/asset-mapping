The problem

The network has built more than it knows it has.

Hub city affiliates have developed strategies, tools, frameworks, and case studies over years of faithful work. Most of it is invisible to the rest of the network — not because it is not shareable, but because there is no system for surfacing it.

What is happening now

Resources exist in silos

City strategies, gospel DNA tools, movement metrics, leadership frameworks, and coaching materials sit in individual affiliate folders, email threads, and local drives — built well, but invisible beyond the city that made them.

What this costs the network

Affiliates rebuild what already exists

Without visibility, cities reinvent. Translation work is duplicated. Gaps in the network go undetected. Bridge resources — the ones that connect clusters across streams — are never identified.

The goal of asset mapping is not to build a new system first and populate it after. It is to go to the people, understand what they have built and what they call it, and let the infrastructure grow from that understanding.

The founding commitment
This is not a knowledge management system imposed on affiliates. It is a GHT initiative, shaped collaboratively — designed before it is built.

From the GHT project brief. The discipline that governs every decision in this pathway.

Governance

The system serves those who built it.

Four principles that govern every decision in this infrastructure.

1

Affiliate ownership

Affiliates retain ownership of all submitted resources. Files stay in affiliate-controlled Google Drive. The system holds metadata and links only — never the file itself.

2

Permission by default

Nothing becomes more visible without explicit approval at the point of submission. No resource is shared beyond its stated permission level — ever.

3

Withdrawal is always possible

Affiliates can request removal of any resource at any time. The system does not hold what people no longer wish to share.

4

Participation is invited, not required

The value of the network grows with participation. It does not depend on universal compliance. No affiliate is pressured to contribute.

The pathway

Asset Mapping Proposal

A three-phase process. Each phase has a clear completion condition before the next begins. Diamonds are decisions — every branch leads somewhere. The sequence is not negotiable: relationship and alignment precede discovery.

What we will not trade away

Our commitments.

Relationship before process

No affiliate outreach before stakeholder MAT/GLT alignment is genuine. No discovery before affiliate leaders are willing co-designers. The process cannot be shortcut by enthusiasm.

Affiliate ownership is not symbolic

Files never leave affiliate-controlled storage. The system holds links. If this changes for any reason — technical or operational — the principle has been violated, not adapted.

Gaps are data too

An affiliate with nothing ready is not a failure of the process. It is a near-zero node in the network graph — an identified under-resourced area. The system is designed to surface gaps, not hide them.

No fixed launch date

This initiative is process-driven, not date-driven. A deadline that forces the co-design to be bypassed produces a system the network did not shape — and will not use.

MAT and GLT alongside affiliates

MAT and GLT are engaged as partners in this process, not as gatekeepers above it. They are briefed at the same time as affiliates, not before. Their role is to support the work — and to raise a genuine blocker only if one exists.

The posture

We are not building a system and then asking people to use it.

We are going to the people first — understanding what they have built, what they call it, and what they are willing to share. The infrastructure grows from that understanding. The value of what is built depends entirely on the trust of the people who built it.

This document is a starting point, not a specification. It will be revised as the co-design process proceeds.
